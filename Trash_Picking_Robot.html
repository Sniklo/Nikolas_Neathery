Making WALL-E – Autonomous Litter Collecting Robot

Master’s Thesis | Advanced Robot Systems | University of Southern Denmark (2025)

Developed a fully functional outdoor autonomous robot capable of detecting, navigating to, and collecting litter in public environments such as parks, sidewalks, and open fields. The system integrates mechanical design, embedded electronics, computer vision, and autonomous navigation within a ROS2-based architecture.

Project Overview

WALL-E was designed to address the growing need for automated environmental cleanup by combining real-time perception, terrain-capable mobility, and an active collection mechanism. The robot operates across asphalt, pavement, grass, and inclines up to 20°, while maintaining high collection reliability.

Key Contributions

Mechanical & Hardware Design

  • Designed and built a four-wheel drivetrain capable of handling uneven outdoor terrain.
  • Engineered a dual counter-rotating brush collection system with an adaptive arm mechanism.
  • Implemented gas-spring counterbalancing for passive load support and vibration resilience.
  • 98.4% litter collection success rate across 15 diverse trash types.

Electronics & Embedded Systems

  • Designed custom 12V power distribution system with voltage regulation and fuse protection.
  • Integrated CIM and 775pro motors with CAN-based motor controllers.
  • Sensor stack: Ouster 3D LiDAR, IMU, encoders, and OAK-D Lite AI camera.
  • Dual Raspberry Pi architecture for distributed control and perception.

Computer Vision & AI

  • Trained and fine-tuned YOLOv11n model for real-time litter detection.
  • mAP@0.5: 93.7%
  • F1 score: 0.88
  • Two-stage domain adaptation pipeline for real-world robustness.
  • ~3.3 ms total inference time for real-time edge deployment.

Software & Autonomy

  • Full ROS2-based modular architecture.
  • SLAM Toolbox + 3D LiDAR for mapping and localization.
  • Manual override using TwistMux and Xbox controller.
  • Simulation in Gazebo + Nav2 for testing and validation.

Technologies Used

ROS2 · Nav2 · SLAM Toolbox · YOLOv11 · Python · C++ · CAN Bus · Raspberry Pi · Ouster LiDAR · OAK-D · Gazebo · Fusion 360 · CNC Manufacturing

Outcome

Successfully delivered a modular proof-of-concept autonomous litter collection platform that demonstrates high detection accuracy, robust terrain traversal, and reliable trash pickup. The system establishes a scalable foundation for future fully autonomous environmental robotics solutions.
