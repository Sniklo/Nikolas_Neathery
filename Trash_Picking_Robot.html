Trash Collecting Robot

overview

For my masters thesis myself and one other student worked to develope a mobile robot from scratch to pick up trash and clutter outdoors. The robot is designed and built from the ground up using a limited budged and limited manufacturing capabilities.

How it works

This project is still in development. For this poject I am responsible for design and fabricated of the robot as well as the hardware integration. The drivetrain was constructed from aluminum extrusion and sheet metal, constrained by limited manufacturing capabilities. To keep costs low due to a restricted budget, hardware included two Raspberry Pis: one for navigation and motor control, and the other for computer vision and AI. Off-the-shelf motor controllers were integrated with the Raspberry Pis via a CAN network, with ROS 2 and Docker ensuring seamless system integration. on the hardware side of things the goal was to keep the robot a cheap as possible. To do this rasperry pis were used for the brains of the robot. Two to be precise one for navigation and motor control and the other for computer vision and AI. ROS2 and docker was used to integrate the two together. The robot also incorperates multiple sensors like encoders, IMUs and lidars. The robot is near the point of being able to drive, the next step of development being the actual trash collection. Some prototyping has been done with scaled down trash and cheap materials like toilet bushes.
