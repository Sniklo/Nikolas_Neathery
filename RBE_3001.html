RBE 3001 – Vision-Based 3DOF Robotic Arm

Overview

RBE 3001 is an advanced robotics course at WPI focused on applying robot kinematics, motion planning, and control theory to real hardware. For the final project, our team developed a vision-guided 3-DOF robotic arm capable of autonomously detecting, picking, and sorting colored balls.

The project required deriving the full forward and inverse kinematics of the manipulator, implementing trajectory planning in joint space, and integrating a camera-based color detection pipeline. All computation and control logic were implemented in MATLAB.

Kinematic Modeling

Forward Kinematics

We derived the homogeneous transformation matrices using the Denavit-Hartenberg convention. This allowed mapping joint angles to end-effector position and orientation in Cartesian space. The full transformation chain was validated experimentally.

Inverse Kinematics

Closed-form inverse kinematics were derived to compute joint angles from a desired end-effector position. Solutions accounted for workspace limits and singular configurations.

Vision System

A camera mounted above the workspace captured images of colored balls. Image processing techniques in MATLAB were used to:

  • Convert RGB to HSV color space
  • Apply threshold-based color segmentation
  • Extract centroids of detected objects
  • Transform image coordinates into robot frame coordinates

Detected object positions were then passed to the inverse kinematics solver to generate pick-and-place trajectories.

Control & Motion Planning

Trajectories were generated in joint space using time-parameterized polynomial interpolation. Position control was implemented for each joint, and the system executed smooth motion sequences for:

  • Approach
  • Grasp
  • Lift
  • Transport
  • Release

The full pick-and-sort routine ran autonomously once initiated, requiring no manual input after startup.
