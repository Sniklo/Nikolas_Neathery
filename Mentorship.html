FRC Mentorship

overview

Looking back I can say that my envolvment with FRC when I was in Highschool is the key reason I am on mt current career path. FRC introduced me to the world of engineering and robotics and since then I cant see myself doing anything else with my career. When I graduated from highschool I took a year to get used to life at college then dove right back into FRC. This time as a mentor. I am eternally grateful for what the program and my mentors did for me and now I am doing my best to pay back that service by mentoring current students on my old team.

How it works

As the lead design mentor for FRC Team 319, Big Bad Bob, I am responsible for teaching students the entire engineering design process. This includes developing designs and prototypes for the annual game and producing a final design using Onshape. While I'm attending college, I conduct virtual strategy and design sessions, as well as CAD learning sessions with the students. In the competition season, once the robot is designed and built, my role shifts to team coach. I assist the students with match strategy and guide them through each match. These roles have enhanced my teaching and leadership skills, while also allowing me to give back to the program that has greatly benefited me.
